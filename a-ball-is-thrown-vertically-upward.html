8. A ball that is thrown vertically upward on the moon return to its starting point in 4 s. Find its original speed?


The original speed = 3.2 m/s²

Further explanation  

The motion of the ball being thrown is included in uniformly accelerated motion with constant acceleration. Its acceleration is influenced by Earth's gravity of g (g = 9.8 m / s²)  

Equation used  

v = u + gt  

v = final velocity

u = initial velocity

g is negative if the motion goes up (vertical upward motion)  

g is positive if the motion goes down (free-fall motion)  

Given

total time = 4 s

Required

The initial speed/u

Solution

The time upward = the time downward so t for upward = 4/2 = 2 s

For the upward journey, at the highest point v = 0, so that :

v = u - gt  

0 = u - gt

u = gt

Assuming the acceleration due to gravity on the moon = 1.6 m/s², and input the value :

[tex]\tt u=1.6\times 2\\\\u=\boxed{\bold{3.2~m/s^2}}[/tex]

9. A ball thrown vertically upward has a speed of 5.0 m/s at a height of 3.0m . The mass of the ball is 1.2kg. Find its mechanical energy.


MECHANICAL ENERGY

Given

Velocity (v) = 5.0 m/sMass (m) = 1.2 kgHeight (h) = 3.0 mAcceleration (g) = 9.8 m/s² (due to gravity)Mechanical Energy (ME) = ?

Calculate the Potential Energy.

PE = mgh

PE = (1.2)(9.8)(3.0)

PE = 35.28 J

Calculate the Kinetic Energy

KE = 1/2mv²

KE = (1/2)(1.2)(5.0)²

KE = (1/2)(1.2)(25.0)

KE = (1/2)(30)

KE = 30/2

KE = 15 J

Calculate the Mechanical Energy

ME = PE + KE

ME = 35.28 J + 15 J

ME = 50.28 J

14. How fast must a ball be thrown vertically upward to reach a height of 12 m?


Answer:

a. The speed of the ball is 15.34 m/s

b. The time for the ball to go back from its original position is 3.13 seconds

Explanation:

In this problem, we are going to use the concept of free-falling bodies to solve the requirements. For the formula, we will use:

a. Vf² = Vi² - 2gy

b. Vf = Vi - gt

where

y     is the height at any given time, the unit is in meter, m

Vi    is the initial velocity, the unit is in m/s

Vf    is the final velocity, the unit is in m/s

t       is the time travelled by the ball, the unit is in seconds, s

g      is the acceleration due to gravity, 9.8 m/s²

Given information

y = 12m

a. Vo = ?

b. t = ?

Solving the problem

a. First, let us solve for the initial velocity of the ball using the given formula

Vf² = Vi² - 2gy

0 = Vi² - 2(9.8 m/s²)(12 m)

Vi =  

Vi = 15.34 m/s

b. Now, let us solve for the time for the ball to go back to its original position.

Vf = Vi - gt

0 = 15.34 m/s - (9.8 m/s²)t

9.8t = 15.34

Cross-multiply

t = 15.34 / 9.8

t = 1.56 seconds

Take note that t = 1.56s is the time travelled by the ball to go up, so let us multiply it by 2 because the time to go up is the same time to go down.

t = 1.56 s x 2

t = 3.13 seconds

Therefore, the upward velocity is 15.34 m/s and the time traveled by the ball to go back to its original position is 3.13 seconds.

18. Two balls are thrown vertically, both at initial speed of 10.0 m/s from height of 60.0 m above the ground. Ball A is thrown upward, while Ball B is projected downward. Which ball will hit the ground first?


Answer:

Ball B will hit the ground first.

Explanation:

In this problem, it is obvious that ball B will hit the ground first. Since they have the same height, ball B is half the time of ball A because ball A will start from throwing up and then goes back to its initial position. Ball A will experience twice the time of its upward throw while ball B will only experience a downward throw.

Now, Let us show some computation to verify the time. The shortest time between the balls indicates which is faster.

Here, we will use the concept of free-falling bodies and solve for the time of each ball.

Given information

Vi = 10 m/s

y = 60 m

t(A) = ?, t (B) = ?

Solving the problem

For Ball A:

Let us use the formula

y = Vit + ¹/₂gt²

60 = 10t + 4.9t²

Simplify to standard form

- 4.9t² - 10t + 60 = 0

Then, using the quadratic equation, let us solve for the time

t = 2.62 s (reject the negative value for time)

Therefore, for the upward throw of ball A, it will take 2.62 seconds to reach a height of 60 meters.

Now, we will multiply the time by 2 because according to the concept of free-falling bodies, the time going up is the same time going down. So,

t(A) = 2.62 s x 2

t(A) = 5.24 s

Therefore, ball A will hit the ground in 5.24 seconds

For Ball B:

Now, let us solve the time for ball B using the same formula

y = Vit + ¹/₂gt²

Then, substitute the given data

60 = 10t + 4.9t²

Simplify to standard form

- 4.9t² - 10t + 60 = 0

Then, using the quadratic equation, let us solve for the time

t(B) = 2.62 s (reject the negative value for time)

Therefore, ball B will take only 2.62 seconds to hit the ground.

Therefore, ball B will hit the ground first.

19. a ball is thrown vertically upward with an initial speed of 80 ft/s. how long will it take for the ball to return to the thrower?​


Answer:

The time for the ball to return to the thrower's hand is 5 seconds.

Explanation:

In this problem, we will be using the concept of free-falling bodies. The formula that we are going to use is:

Vf = Vi - gt

where

Vf     is the final velocity, the given unit is in ft/s

Vi     is the initial velocity, the given unit is also in ft/s

g      is the acceleration due to gravity, the unit to use is 32 ft/s²

        since the given initial speed is in ft/s

t       is the time traveled by the ball

Solving the problem

Let us solve for the time for the ball to go up by using the given formula above.

Vf = Vi - gt

Then substitute the given data

0 = 80 ft/s - (32ft/s²)t

Take note that the final velocity (vertical velocity) at maximum height reached by the ball is zero.

Transpose (32ft/s²)t to the left side of the equation

(32ft/s²)t = 80 ft/s

Divide both sides by 32ft/s²

t = (80 ft/s) / (32ft/s²)

t = 2.5 seconds

Now, to compute the time for the ball to return to the thrower's hand, we will multiply 2.5  seconds by 2 because the time going up is the same time going down.

Therefore,

t = 2.5 seconds x 2

t = 5 seconds

Therefore, the time for the ball to return to the thrower's hand is 5 seconds.

Answer:

The velocity of the at maximum height is 0 m/s at the highest point since, when you throw a ball upward, it decelerates and stops momentarily at the highest point before it falls back to the ground.

Explanation:

Acceleration due to gravity going up is -9.8 m/s² (decelerating), which means the velocity of the object going up decreases by 9.8 m/s every second and eventually the object will momentarily stops when it reaches it maximum height before if falls back to the ground. The object stops at maximum height due to deceleration brought by gravity. The velocity of the object at maximum height is 0 m/s (it stops before it falls back to the ground).

25. Learning Task 1: In your notebook, define1. Melody2. Rhythm3. Dynamics4. Texture5. TempoLearning Task 2: Open the link or sca​


Answer:

melody- a sequence of single notes that is musically satisfying.

Rhythm- Rhythm, in music, the placement of sounds in time. In its most general sense, rhythm (Greek rhythmos, derived from rhein, “to flow”) is an ordered alternation of contrasting elements.

Dynamics- Dynamics refers to the volume of a sound or note. The term is also applied to the written or printed musical notation used to indicate dynamics. ... Forte means loud and piano means soft.

Texture- texture is how the tempo, melody melodic, and harmony harmonic materials are combined in a musical composition, determining the overall quality of the sound in a piece. 

Tempo- Tempo means the speed at which a piece of music should be played. As with many other musical terms, Italian words are used to describe different tempos of music. Adagio - a slow tempo (other words for slow are lento and largo) Andante - performed at a walking pace.

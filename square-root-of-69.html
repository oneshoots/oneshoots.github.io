1. What is the square root of 69​


Answer:

[tex] \sqrt{69} [/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

Taking square roots manually Factor out the given number until its factors are only prime numbersIf there are prime factors that are repeated (2n) times, divide its exponent by two and place the 'new' number with its 'new' exponent to the left of the radical, outside

Example

[tex] \sqrt{288} = \sqrt{2 \times 144} = \sqrt{2 \times 2 \times 72} = \sqrt{ {2}^{2} \times 2 \times 36 } = \sqrt{ {2}^{2} \times {6}^{2} \times 2 } [/tex]

Notice 2² and 6². Those are the factors with even expinents.

This is where we divide the exponent by 2 then place outside the radical the 'new' number

[tex] \implies \sqrt{( {2}^{ \frac{2}{2} } )( {6}^{ \frac{2}{2} } )2} [/tex]

[tex](2 {}^{ \frac{2}{2} } )(6 {}^{ \frac{2}{2} } ) \sqrt{2} = 2(6) \sqrt{2} = 12 \sqrt{2} [/tex]

(I extracted the other 2 for it would be more complicated to explain how to get the root given factors with odd exponents)

-----------------------------------------------------

[tex] \sqrt{69} = \sqrt{3 \times 13} [/tex]

Notice that we are left with prime factors.

This means the square root of 69 is √69 in radicals.

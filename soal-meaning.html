1. Body Odor

2. Postural Changes

- We should always practice proper posture

in everything we do, or else we might

develop postural problems

3. Growth of body hair

- In boys, they can shave body hairs that

are visible. But in some areas, like the

pubic hair, it is not recommended to

shave it. Body hair is normal and we

shouldn't ve embarrassed of it.

B. MENTAL CHANGE

1. Suicidal Thoughts

- This is a very serious health concern.

It requires the help of professionals and

unconditional help and understanding of

loved ones.

2. Insecurities

- Some adolescents are insecured, but

always remind them that it is normal and

they are beautiful in their own ways.

3. Risky behaviors

- Rebellion is one of the common concerns

for teenagers. Thus, parents should show

support and prohibition in balanced

quantities.

C. Emotional Changes

1. Sensitivity

-In this stage of their lives, they are much

more sensitive. Small actions can cause

them to cry, laugh or be mad. Just ve

careful when you're with them, knowing

that they are going through a lot.

2. Mood Swings

- From being happy to sad and to angry,

Sometimes they feel all those emotions at

the same time. Their moods change

frequently. They just need to be

understood and accepted.

3. Stress or Depression

- Because of the pressure in home or

school, some feel stressed or even

depressed. They need to rest for a while. If

it is serious, They need professional help.

D. Social Changes

1. Social Withdrawal

- They distance themself from others,

maybe because of shyness or

embarassment. Help them socialize, and

be more open to others.

2. Become closer to friends

-They value friendships more than family.

It is normal, but you should help them

reconnect with their family

3. Socially incapable

- It is rarely happens, but a few teens

become afraid to socialize. They should

always be reminded that it is also

important to from new relationships.

E. Moral/Spiritual Changes

1. Does not go to church

- Most adolescents don't go to church with

their family anymore. They should be

reminded that God is still a priority.

2. Bully

-Some learn to bully and tease others. They

should be disciplined and reprimanded.

3. No cooperation

-They don't cooperate in group or

community projects. They should be

convinced that cooperating and helping

others can be educational and considered

a self-growth.

F. Sexual Changes

1. Form Romantic relationships

-Most teenagers start to like fellow

teenagers and form relationshio with them.

It isn't a concern, still they should be aware

not to trust too easily.

2. Coming Out

-The true gender identity of a person

really comes out. They need to be

accepted and understood.

3. Gender Confusion

-Some who still aren't sure with their

gender are confused. They need support

and assurance that they will be accepted

whoever they choose to be.

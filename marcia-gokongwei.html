John Robinson Lim Gokongwei Jr. was a Filipino billionaire businessman and philanthropist. He had holdings in telecommunications, financial services, petrochemicals, power generation, aviation, food, beverage, and livestock farming.

23. (Write Informal English or Academic English): Marcia is unpredictable.


Answer:

Can we classify the written sentence as having an informal format or more of an Academic English structure?

The sentence “Marcia is unpredictable” is closer to what we may refer to as informal English, because to me at least, the word “unpredictable” when describing someone sounds like a cliche. Calling someone “unpredictable” is also a personal opinion which no one can use in academic writing. So by using a cliche while expressing a personal opinion, the writer or the speaker made a sentence that aligns more towards what most will say is Informal English.

Explanation:

Here are what is allowed in informal English:  

The use of Contractions  Phrasal verbs Using slang words Idioms Negatives  Pronouns Cliches Vague language  You can use simple vocabulary Punctuation (there are certain punctuations do not make it on many academic papers) Personal opinion Direct language

A reminder, all of what is listed above isn’t allowed in Academic English which distinguishes itself as being more formal. If you need a clear picture of how Academic English is written or spoken, you can read articles written by or listen to speeches by University Professors and Intellectuals. Watching documentaries narrated by an expert is probably the simplest way to get a grasp of what Academic English is.  

As mentioned, being formal prevents the Academic writer from using words like the following:

Isn’t Don’t Aren’t

They would replace these contractions with “is not, do not, are not”.

An academic writer would use feline instead of the word cat, the word children would be used instead of kids. Slang and Cliches are hardly ever used in any formal writing.

Objectivity while presenting a view is the entire idea of Academic English, and avoiding those on the list above, especially the injecting one’s personal opinion forces the writer to present references to validate a point.  

This is very different from informal English as it is clearly used in everyday conversations with anybody, the topics are open and you can freely express your subjectivity. In written form, informal English hosts many of the punctuations, even the exclamation point (!) which is nearly absent in all academic writings.

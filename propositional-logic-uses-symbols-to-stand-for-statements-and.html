16. 1. Logically equivalent propositions are _____​


The propositions are equal or logically equivalent if they always have the same truth value. That is p and q are logically equivalent if p is true whenever q is true, and vice versa, and if p is false whenever q is false,and vice versa. If p and q are logically equivalent we write p=q.

Instead of elementary algebra, where the values of the variables are numbers and the prime operations are addition and multiplication, the main operations of Boolean algebra are the conjunction (and) denoted as ∧, the disjunction (or) denoted as ∨, and the negation (not) denoted as ¬.

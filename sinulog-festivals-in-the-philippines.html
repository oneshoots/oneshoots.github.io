5. What are the instruments used in the Philippine festivals? (Ati-Atihan, Sinulog, Kadayawan, Ibalong, Masskara, Moriones, Sublian)​


Ati-atihan - drums, trumpets, xylophones, gongs, and lyres.

Sinulog - drums, trumpets, and the native gongs.

Kadayawan - kulintang ensemble.

Ibalong - chants, kalee drum, gong, tong atong, bamboo percussions, violin, jembe, chimes, tiboli bells, balimbing.

Masskara - Tongatong (bamboo instruments) of Kalingan, voice ad libs called “Tata” by Panay Bukidnon, Palawan bamboo and gong counter rhythm, Babandir gongs of Maguindanao, and big Taiko drum accents from Japan.26

Moriones - clarinet, brass instrument, timpani, concert band.

Sublian - drums and chanting.

Answer:

The Sinulog-Santo Niño Festival is an annual cultural and religious festival held on the third Sunday of January in Cebu City, and on the fourth Sunday of January in Carmen, Cebu, and is the centre of the Santo Niño Catholic celebrations in the Philippines.

The word “Sinulog” means “graceful dance”, wherein it all started in 1980 with a simple dance that represents the “sulog” (or current) of a river in Cebu. With the celebration's evolution, the Sinulog Festival has become a month long tradition in Cebu

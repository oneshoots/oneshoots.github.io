24. What are the missing coefficients for the skeleton equation below? NH3 (g) + O2(g) → N2(g) + H2O(l)​


The balanced equation :

4NH₃ (g) + 3O₂(g) → 2N₂(g) + 6H₂O(l)​

Further explanation

A chemical reaction can be expressed in terms of:  

words equation the skeleton equation  balanced equation

The skeleton equation is still in unbalanced form  

so only chemical formulas of reactants and products so that the relative amounts of the reactants and products are not included

Reaction

NH₃ (g) + O₂(g) → N₂(g) + H₂O(l)​

Give a coefficient to balance equation :

NH₃ (g) + aO₂(g) → bN₂(g) + cH₂O(l)​

Make a equation :

N, left=1, right=2b⇒2b=1⇒b=1/2

H, left=3, right=2c⇒2c=3⇒c=3/2

O, left=2a, right=c⇒2a=c⇒2a=3/2⇒a=3/4

The equation becomes :

NH₃ (g) + 3/4O₂(g) → 1/2N₂(g) + 3/2H₂O(l) ​x4

4NH₃ (g) + 3O₂(g) → 2N₂(g) + 6H₂O(l)​

The first, second, third, fourth, fifth, and sixth positions are the five most important positions of dance.

Folk dancing has five fundamental positions.The First Position. The feet are straightened outward and the heels are maintained together. Raise arms in front of the chest in a circle.The second Position. One foot separates the feet, and they are straight outward in a straight line. Sideways open arms with a lovely curvature lifted below shoulder level.Third position. The heels are positioned together, one in front of the other, and the feet are turned outward. Raise one arm aloft while keeping the other in the second position.Position number four. If you drew a line from one foot's heel to the other's toes, you'd have the sides of a square. The feet are separated by one foot and turned outward with one foot in front of the other, parallel to each other.In the fifth spot. A foot is placed squarely in front of the other on either side of the body, and the big toe's initial joint reaches past each heel.The Five Fundamental Positions The five basic positions are suitably named: first position, second position, third position, fourth position, and fifth position, and they concern the location of the feet.What is dance?Dance is the art of mastering the rhythmically coordinated and expressive movement.

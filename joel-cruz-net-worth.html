20. Mr. Cruz owns half of the company and his best friend owns the other half. If Mr. Cruz would give 250,000 pesos from his share of company to his best friend, Mr. Cruz would own only one-third of the company. What is the total worth of the company?


Answer:

To find the total worth of the company, you need to determine how much Mr. Cruz owns before giving his friend 250,000 pesos and how much his friend owns.

If Mr. Cruz owns half of the company and he gives 250,000 pesos to his friend, he would own only one-third of the company. This means that the total worth of the company is equal to three times the amount that Mr. Cruz owns before giving his friend the 250,000 pesos.

To solve for the total worth of the company, you can set up the following equation:

Total worth of company = 3 * (Mr. Cruz's share before giving 250,000 pesos)

Since Mr. Cruz owns half of the company, his share before giving his friend 250,000 pesos is equal to half of the total worth of the company. You can substitute this expression into the equation above to get:

Total worth of company = 3 * (1/2 * Total worth of company)

Solving this equation gives you the total worth of the company:

Total worth of company = 750,000 pesos

So, the total worth of the company is 750,000 pesos.

Let X be the total worth of the company.

Since Mr. Cruz owns half of the company, his share is worth X/2.

After Mr. Cruz gives 250,000 pesos to his best friend, he will own only one-third of the company, so his share will be worth X/3.

The total worth of the company is equal to the sum of Mr. Cruz's share and his best friend's share, or X/2 + X/2 = X.

Therefore, X/3 = X/2 - 250,000.

Solving for X, we find that X = 750,000 pesos.

Therefore, the total worth of the company is [tex]750,000[/tex] pesos.

Since Q is in the midpoint, it means that you are going to place the sticker Q in the middle of the stick.

28. ∆PQR where PQ=9,QR=6 and PR=5 Solutions: To find m∠P (QR)²=(PR)²+(PQ)²-2(PR)(PQ) (cosP)


✏️LAW OF COSINES==============================

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{PROBLEM:}} [/tex] ∆PQR where PQ=9,QR=6 and PR=5

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{SOLUTION:}} [/tex] To find m∠P

[tex] \small \sf (QR)^2 = (PR)^2 + (PQ)^2 - 2(PR)(PQ)(cos \: P) [/tex]

[tex] \sf 6^2 = 5^2 + 9^2 - 2(5)(9)(cos \: P) [/tex]

[tex] \sf 36 = 25 + 81 - 90(cos \: P) [/tex]

[tex] \sf cos \: P = \frac{25 + 81 - 36}{90} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf cos \: P = \frac{70}{90} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf m \angle P = cos^{-1} \Big( \frac{70}{90} \Big) \\ [/tex]

[tex] \sf m \angle P = 38.94 \degree \\ [/tex]

[tex] \large \therefore \underline{\boxed{\tt \purple{m \angle P = 38.94 \sf \degree}}} [/tex]

What is m∠2, if m∠7 = 102?

Choices:

788892102

Notice m∠2 and m∠7 are same exterior angles. Same exterior angles are exterior angles on the same side of a transversal. These angles are supplementary.

Since these 2 angles are supplementary, let's equate them to 180.

[tex]\underline{\bold{Solution:}}[/tex]

[tex]\tt{x+102=180}\\\tt{x=180-102}\\\tt{x=78}[/tex]

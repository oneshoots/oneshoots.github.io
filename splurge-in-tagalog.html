3. Joaquin can't control the urge to splurge on things he does not need and often runs into financial problems. What disorder does he have?


If Joaquin cannot control the urge to splurge on things he does not need and often runs into financial problems, then he has compulsive buying disorder. This is otherwise known as compulsive shopping disorder. The symptoms include being preoccupied with buying, impairment on daily activities to this pre-occupation, manic or hypomanic episodes of buying. A compulsive buyers also is typically dissatisfied with what was bough and repeating the cycle of buying. It is a serious disease because it can also result to financial problems or run-ins with the law.

This disorder is closesly related to other compulsive diseases such as OCD and impulsive eating. It is best to consulat a therapist or a psychiatrist when experiencing this. The typical modalies of treatment include talk theraphy and medications for depression and other diseases.

BUDGET

- MOST EFFECTIVE WAY TO HANDLE MONEY

-a list of items that one plans to spend in a given time, given income or amount.

- it is a plan for spending and also plan for saving and spending for the future

IMPORTANCE OF A BUDGET

1. Balance your income and your expenditures

2. Give a feeling of comfort and satisfaction knowing your income is enough for the expenses of your family

3. Enable the family to list down their needs and wants

4. Give family a full and better life

5. Have a constant reminder that you are in control of family finances

TYPES OF SPENDING

1. SPENDING FOR COMFORTABLE LIVING

- spending money for basic items that one needs from day to day like foods, clothing, transportation

2. SPENDING FOR MAJOR PURCHASES-

- spending money for personal budget like buying new gadgets

3. SPENDING FOR SAVINGS

- spending that is rewarding bec. one is assured of extra cash in the future espc. in time of emergency .

4. SPLURGE SPENDING

-pending for fun ad satisfaction

FACTORS AFFECTING FAMILY BUDGET

1. AMOUNT OF INCOME

- the higher the income of the family the greater the amount the family members can spend for their needs and wants

2. SIZE AND COMPOSITION OF THE FAMILY

- the more family members are there, the bigger amount of income is spent on food, schooling , clothing and for their basic needs.

3. VALUES OF THE FAMILY

- what does the family consider most important in the budget?

- it pertains to the priorities on what to spend their money for.

4. NEEDS AND WANTS OF THE FAMILY

- spending for the important for the comfortable living vs. spending what most families want to buy

- remember prioritise the needs of the family before the wants.

NEEDS

- are materials which the family members need to survive on day to day basis

WANTS

- materials that the family members desire to have

STEPS IN MAKING BUDGET

1. Know your income or allowance for definite period of time

2. Make a list of expenses

3. Determine the amount to be spent on fixed and flexible expenses.

4. Add expenses for the 2 categories

5. Check on the figure to find out if you are spending the right amount where you need to.

6. Do your total expenses balance with your income.

CLASSIFICATION OF EXPENSES

1. FIXED EXPENSES

- expenses that are set aside for daily expenses of family for food, transportation, allowance and others

2. FLEXIBLE EXPENSES

- amount may be increase or decrease depending on the money available. example: clothing, gifts , recreation

3. OCCASIONAL AND EMERGENCY EXPENSES

- amount may be considered s form of savings for health and emergency espenses and special occasions

Negative correlation occurs when an increase in one variable is associated with a decrease in the other variable, and a decrease in one variable is associated with an increase in the other.

Zero or no correlation occurs when there is no relationship between the two variables, meaning that a change in one variable does not have any effect on the other.

27. Make Your Own Story (Any kind of the lvls of Biological Organization) ​


Once upon a time, a group of cells decided to come together and form a multicellular organism. They knew that by working together, they could perform complex functions that single cells could not do on their own.

The cells organized themselves into tissues, with each tissue specializing in a specific function. For example, some cells formed the skin, which protected the organism from the outside world, while others formed the muscles, which allowed the organism to move.

The tissues organized themselves into organs, with each organ performing a specific function. For example, the heart pumped blood throughout the body, while the lungs helped the organism breathe.

The organs then organized themselves into systems, with each system performing a specific set of functions. For example, the digestive system helped the organism break down food and extract nutrients, while the nervous system helped the organism sense and respond to its environment.

As the organism grew and matured, it became a vital member of its ecosystem, interacting with other organisms and the environment around it. It was able to survive and thrive thanks to the intricate organization of its cells, tissues, organs, and systems.

And that, my friends, is the power of the different levels of biological organization

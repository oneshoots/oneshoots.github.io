4. Eric is ten years younger than Jason. In six years, Jason will be twice as old as Eric. What are their present ages?​


Answer:

Eric is 4 years old and Jason is 14 years old.

Step-by-step explanation:

Age Word Problem

Let us analyze the problem.

What is asked?

The present ages of Eric and Jason.

What are the given?

Let x be Jason's age.

Present Ages:

Jason - x

Eric - x - 10

(ten years younger than Jason)

In six years:

Eric - (x - 10) + 6

Jason - x + 6

- 2(x - 10) + 6

(twice as old as Eric)

We need to add 6 because these are their ages in 6 years.

What is the number sentence?

x + 6 = 2(x - 10) + 6

We have this equation, from Jason's age because we need to find his age first, for us to find Eric's.

What is the solution?

Solve. Combine similar terms.

Jason's Age in 6 Years:

x + 6 = 2(x - 10) + 6

x + 6 = 2(x - 10) + 6

x + 6 = 2x - 20 + 6

x + 6 = 2x - 14

14 + 6 = 2x - x

20 = x

In 6 years, Jason will be 20 years old.

Now, that we know Jason's age in 6 years, we can now find their present ages.

Jason's Present Age:

To find his present age, take away 6 from his age in 6 years.

20 - 6 = 14

Jason is 14 years old.

Eric's Present Age:

x = 14

x - 10

14 - 10 = 4

Eric is 4 years old.

Checking:

Let us check if our answers meet the given condition.

"In six years, Jason will be twice as old as Eric."

Eric - 4 + 6 = 10 years old

Jason - 20 years old

Yes! 20 is twice of 10. ✔

Age Word Problem

Age word problems are algebra word problems that deal with the ages of people currently, in the past or in the future. They involve ages of one person or two or more persons.

Important Statements and Equations for "Problems based on Ages":

Answering age word problems may be difficult for some. Here are some of the important tips that can help in formulating the equations.

If the present age is y, then n times the present age = ny.

If the present age is x, then age n years later/hence = x + n.

If the present age is x, then age n years ago = x – n.

18. A to H are persons compared according to their age and speed at which they finish a certain job. A is older than B and faster than C. D is younger than E, older that H, and slower than F. G is older than H, younger than C, slower than H, and faster than F. H is older than A and slower than C. Based on the preceding statements, which of the following is true? a. A is the second oldest and the third fastest. b. G is older than E and slower than A. c. C is younger than D and fastest than H. d. D is the eldest and slowest. e. H is the older than B and slower than D.


Let us separate the problem into two parts, one focusing on age and the other on speed.

Whenever it would be stated in the solution that A > B or something of the like, this would mean that A is older than B or that A is faster than B.


Part 1: Age

A is older than B. (A > B)
D is younger than E, older than H. (D < E, D > H)
G is older than H, younger than C. (G > H, G < C)
H is older than A. (H > A)

Combine all facts / inequalities,

G, D > H > A > B <---------- since more than one person is older than A,                                                      option a is invalid.
E > D <-------- since D is younger than E, option d is false.
C > G

We are then left with options b, c and e.


Part 2: Speed

A is faster than C. (A > C)
D is slower than F. (D < F)
G is slower than H, faster than F. (G < H, G > F)
H is slower than C. (H < C)

Again, combine all facts / inequalities,

A > C > H > G > F > D 
Option e is false as H is faster than D.

We are then left with options b and c which are both possible, but not surely true as there are no statements that would confirm that G is older than E or that C is younger than D.

Note: that is if option c really means that c is faster than h, but if it would be a typo and that c is fastest, option b would be correct

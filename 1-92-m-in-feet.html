13. The volume of a pyramid varies jointly as its height and the area of its base. A pyramid with a height of 12feet and a base with area of 23 square feet has a volume of 92 cubic feet. Find the volume of a pyramid with a height of 17 feet and a base with an area of 27 square feet


Volume varies jointly with its height and area of the base

V = khb where k is a constant


Solve k:

When h = 12 ft and b = 23 ft², v = 92 ft³

92 - k(12)(23)

k = 92 ÷ 276

k = 1/3


=> Volume = 1/3 x height x base area


Find volume when height = 17 ft and area = 27 ft²

Volume = 1/3 x height x base area

Volume = 1/3 x 17 x 27 = 153 ft³


Answer: The volume is 153 ft³

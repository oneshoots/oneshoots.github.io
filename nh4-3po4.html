5. Calculate the boiling point elevation in oC of an aqueous solution of 18.73% by mass of (NH4)3PO4. Express your answer in decimal form in 4 significant figures, not in scientific notation. Kb of H2O = 0.512 oC/m molar masses: H2O = 18.015 g/mol (NH4)3PO4= 149.087 g/mol


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the mass of solute [(NH₄)₃PO₄].

Assume that the mass of solution is 100 g.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of solute} & = \frac{\text{percent by mass}}{100\%} \times \text{mass of solution} \\ & = \frac{18.73\%}{100\%} \times \text{100 g} \\ & = \text{18.73 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mass of solvent (water).

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of solvent} & = \text{mass of solution} - \text{mass of solute} \\ & = \text{100 g} - \text{18.73 g} \\ & = \text{81.27 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of solute} & = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}} \\ & = \frac{\text{18.73 g}}{\text{149.087 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.12563 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the molality of the solution.

The mass of solvent must be expressed in kilograms. 81.27 g = 0.08127 kg

[tex]\begin{aligned} m & = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.12563 mol}}{\text{0.08127 kg}} \\ & = \text{1.5458 mol/kg} \\ & = 1.5458 \: m \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Determine the van't Hoff factor.

Since (NH₄)₃PO₄ is a strong electrolyte, it will dissociate completely into three NH₄⁺ and one PO₄³⁻ ions. Then, the van't Hoff factor is

i = 4

Step 6: Calculate the boiling-point elevation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \Delta T_{\text{b}} & = iK_{\text{b}}m \\ & = (4)(0.512^{\circ}\text{C/}m)(1.5458 \: m) \\ & = \boxed{3.166^{\circ}\text{C}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the boiling point elevation of an aqueous solution of 18.73% by mass (NH₄)₃PO₄ is 3.166°C.

Answer:

mass = 83 g

Explanation:

Determining the van't Hoff factor

Assume 100% dissociation. Ammonium phosphate will dissociate into 3 ammonium ions and 1 phosphate ion. Adding 3 and 1

i = 3 + 1 = 4

Calculating the freezing point depression

∆Tf = FP solvent - FP solution

∆Tf = 0°C - (-8.3°C)

∆Tf = 8.3°C

Calculating the molality of the solution

∆Tf = i

molality = ∆Tf / (kb • i)

molality = 8.3°C / (1.86°C/m • 4)

molality = 1.1156 m

Calculate the number of moles of ammonium phosphate

n = molality • kg solvent

n = 1.1156 mol/kg • 0.500 kg

n = 0.5578 mol

Calculate the mass of ammonium phosphate

mass = 0.5578 mol × (149.09 g / 1 mol)

mass = 83 g

17. Ammonium phosphate (NH4)3PO4 is a compound that is often used by farmers as fertilizers. If Mang Ambo has 100 grams of this compound. Can you calculate its molecular mass, numbers of moles, and its number of particles? Write your answer on a separate sheet of paper. -need na po now pls


Answer:

To calculate the molecular mass of a compound, you need to add up the atomic masses of all the atoms in its chemical formula.

The molecular formula of Ammonium Phosphate is (NH4)3PO4 . The molecular mass of (NH4)3PO4 is :

(NH4)3PO4 : 114 + 41 + 331 + 116 + 416 = 3(14 + 4 + 31) + 1*(16 + 4) = 3*(49) + 1*20 = 147

To find the number of moles in 100 grams of Ammonium Phosphate, you can use the formula:

moles = mass / molar mass

where mass is the mass of the substance in grams and molar mass is the molecular mass of the substance in g/mol.

So, moles = 100 / 147 = 0.68

To find the number of particles (molecules) in 0.68 moles of Ammonium Phosphate, you can use Avogadro's number which is 6.022 x 10^23 particles per mole.

Number of particles = moles x Avogadro's number

So, Number of particles = 0.68 x 6.022 x 10^23 = 4.08 x 10^23

Therefore, 100 grams of Ammonium Phosphate has a molecular mass of 147 g/mol, contains 0.68 moles and 4.08 x 10^23 particles.

Sequence of events in the story Orpheus

Initiating Events

Orpheus was known to play the lyre, that even monsters would listen to him play, forgetting they are scary creatures. He loved a woman called Eurydice. They married. Unfortunately, his wife was killed by a poisonous snake when she stepped on it.

Second Events

Orpheus became really lonely after her death, playing sad songs on his lyre to move on. He did everything to forget what happened but it was stuck in his mind. So he decided to visit the Underworld to bring Eurydice back.

Third Events

As he went through the passage, he passed a lot of obstacles, but because he was gifted musically with the lyre, he entered Hades' kingdom effortlessly. Finally, he arrived and convinced Hades and Persephone to bring back his wife with the lyre. However, Hades had a task for him: he cannot look at his wife until they finally exit.

Outcome

As they exit, Orpheus called out to Eurydice following him but she didn't answer. He saw a glimpse of her arm and she disappeared, swallowed by the darkness of the passageway.

(Plot of the story: brainly.ph/question/2118555)

The story A Shawl for Anita is written by Lolita Andrada that shows a family conflict inflicted by a misunderstood reason and an envious mind. The story shown that Anita was demanding her mother to finish a shawl for her even at her mother's weak and frail state. But in the end the Anita was doing her mother a favor since by making her mother knit a shawl for her, she is giving her a reason to hold on to life.

To know the theme of the story A Shawl For Anita please visit this brainly.ph/question/2108052

5 Best Events in the Story "A Shawl for Anita by Lolita Andrada:The middle child stated that their mother raised them single-handed. That her mother deal with 3 Adolescent girls, The eldest, the narrator, and Anita, the youngest. In this scene the story was given a mood that the family is poor and that there will be a conflict since she stated that they are 3 adolescent girls.Next is when the middle child mention that "she never finished high school, but her deft hands had skillfully eked out a living for the four of us. She was good at knitting." This gives us the point of view that their mother is really hardworking and that she will be the one knitting the shawl for Anita.When the narrator tells that: "Mother had a soft heart - especially for Anita." By hearing/reading this we already know that her mother are fond of the youngest of the three, which is Anita. And this statement tells the reader that the middle child is now beginning the conflict within herself.When the narrator stated that "My heart went out to her every time I saw her painfully the knitting needles into the yarn." With this she outburst and told Anita what she really feels. This gives us an impression of courage and bravery to speak to your own family about the thing that bothers you the most. This is the climax of the story.When the middle child learns about the truth about the reason for knitting Anita's shawl. This is a very touching part wherein I, personally almost cried. For here we learned that her mother only lives up to that time because of Anita's shawl. And that resolves all the conflict within the family of 4-girls in one roof.

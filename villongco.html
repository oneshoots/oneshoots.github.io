1. differences of illonggo and cebuano culture​


CULTURE OF ILONGGO

Iloilo is also host to a very rich and colorful culture brought about by a very eventful past. The essential components of Ilonggo culture are language, oral literature (epics, myths, legends, proverbs, etc.), songs and dances, handicrafts, old churches and houses, and famous delicacies.

TRADITION OF ILONGGO

Ilonggos enjoy a more laid-back lifestyle, where traffic is barely an issue. Everyone knows one another and can often trace their own and each other's family trees, based on the stories and tales of grandparents. Citizens of Iloilo and Bacolod love good company, good ambiance, and a good meal.5 J

CULTURE OF CEBUANO

They are proud of their being artistic and creative; various forms of art (painting, sculpting, and drawing), singing, dancing, and writing are cornerstones of Cebuano culture. Many popular mainstream artists in the Philippines are from Cebu City, and painters, singers, and writers reside there.

TRADITION OF CEBUANO

The Cebuanos observe certain customs and traditions about birth, baptism, courtship, marriage, death and burial.

23. if the object distance is equal to twice the focal length, how do the distances and sizes of the image and the object compare?advance thank u sa answer!​


Answer:

If the object distance (d_o) is equal to twice the focal length (f) of a lens or mirror, the image is at the lens's principal focus (F'), and the image is real, inverted, and has the same size as the object.

This is known as the "2f" or "back focal length" rule, which states that the distance between the lens (or mirror) and the image will be twice the focal length of the lens if the object is placed at its principal focus. This is true for all types of lenses and mirrors, including convex lenses and concave mirrors.

The image distance is equal to d_i = 2f and since the Magnification(M) = -(d_i / d_o) = -(2f / 2f) = -1 and it means the image is of same size but inverted.

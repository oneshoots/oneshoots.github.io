2. Describe the history and backround of ballroom dance and cha cha​


Answer:

Ballroom dance is a type of dance that is performed by couples and is typically done to ballroom music. It has a long history and evolved from folk dance in the 18th and 19th centuries. The first ballroom dance competitions were held in the late 19th century. The most common ballroom dances are Waltz, Tango, Foxtrot, Quickstep, and Viennese Waltz.

Cha-cha dance is a ballroom dance that originated in Cuba in the 1950s. It is a lively, upbeat dance that is characterized by its syncopated rhythm and Latin flavor. It was introduced in the United States in the early 1950s and became popular in ballroom dance competitions and social dance events.

25. Activity. Research Direction: Explain the types of Ballroom Dances. 1. Cha-cha 2. Rumba 3. Jive 4. Paso Doble 5. Viennese Waltz​


Answer:

1. The cha-cha-cha, is a dance of Cuban origin. It is danced to the music of the same name introduced by Cuban composer and violinist Enrique Jorrin in the early 1950s. This rhythm was developed from the danzón-mambo.

2. Rhumba, also known as ballroom rumba, is a genre of ballroom music and dance that appeared in the East Coast of the United States during the 1930s. It combined American big band music with Afro-Cuban rhythms, primarily the son cubano, but also conga and rumba.

3. The jive is a dance style that originated in the United States from African Americans in the early 1930s. The name of the dance comes from the name of a form of African-American vernacular slang, popularized in the 1930s by the publication of a dictionary by Cab Calloway, the famous jazz bandleader and singer.

4. Pasodoble is a fast-paced Spanish military march used by infantry troops. Its speed allowed troops to give 120 steps per minute. This march gave rise to a traditional Spanish dance, a musical genre including both voice and instruments, and a genre of instrumental music often played during bullfight.

5. Viennese waltz is a genre of ballroom dance. At least four different meanings are recognized. In the historically first sense, the name may refer to several versions of the waltz, including the earliest waltzes done in ballroom dancing, danced to the music of Viennese waltz.

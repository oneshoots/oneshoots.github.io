1. description of kotsuzumi​


Answer:

The kotsuzumi is a small, portable drum used in Nō, nagauta (dance music), geza (off-stage Kabuki theater music), and traditional folk music. The left hand holds the kotsuzumi up to the right shoulder and squeezes the rope, while the right hand strikes the head in various ways.

Answer:

The kotsuzumi is a double-headed hourglass-shaped membranophone of Japan. Its primary contexts of use are in the nō (noh) drama hayashi (ensemble), the kabuki theatre (for both the onstage nagauta or debayashi ensemble and the offstage geza ensemble), and some folk music genres.

7. 1.what is kotsuzumi?2.what is shamisen?3.what is Fue?4.what is otsuzumi?5.What is Taiko?​


Answer:

Kotsuzumi

The tsudzumi or tsuzumi is a hand drum of Japanese origin. It consists of a wooden body shaped like an hourglass, and it is taut, with two drum heads with cords that can be squeezed or released to increase or decrease the tension of the heads respectively.

Shamisen

The shamisen or samisen, also sangen, is a three-stringed traditional Japanese musical instrument derived from the Chinese instrument sanxian. It is played with a plectrum called a bachi. The Japanese pronunciation is usually shamisen but sometimes jamisen when used as a suffix, according to regular sound change.

Fue

Fue is the Japanese word for bamboo flute, and refers to a class of flutes native to Japan. Fue come in many varieties, but are generally high-pitched and made of a bamboo called shinobue. The most popular of the fue is the shakuhachi.

Otsuzumi

The Ōtsuzumi, also known as the Ōkawa, is an hourglass-shaped Japanese drum. It is a larger version of the tsuzumi, or kotsuzumi and is used in traditional Japanese theater and folk music.

Taiko

Taiko are a broad range of Japanese percussion instruments. In Japanese, the term refers to any kind of drum, but outside Japan, it is used specifically to refer to any of the various Japanese drums called wadaiko and to the form of ensemble taiko drumming more specifically called kumi-daiko.

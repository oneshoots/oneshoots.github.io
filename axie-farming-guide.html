The three types of correlation are positive, negative, and zero or no correlation.

Positive correlation occurs when an increase in one variable is associated with an increase in the other variable, and a decrease in one variable is associated with a decrease in the other.

Negative correlation occurs when an increase in one variable is associated with a decrease in the other variable, and a decrease in one variable is associated with an increase in the other.

Zero or no correlation occurs when there is no relationship between the two variables, meaning that a change in one variable does not have any effect on the other.

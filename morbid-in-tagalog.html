Mortality and morbidity are two related terms usually used in the field of epidemiology. Both mortality and morbidity are used to indicate the frequency and severity of a disease or condition.

The state of having a specific ailment or condition is known as morbidity. While morbidity can relate to an acute ailment, such as a respiratory infection, it's more commonly used to allude to a long-term problem (long-lasting). The following are some instances of common diseases such as diabetes, pressure in the arteries (hypertension), ailment of the heart, obesity, and other severe diseases. Morbidity statistics are frequently given in two formats: incidence and prevalence.

The term "mortality" refers to the number of people who have died as a result of a particular illness or ailment. The term "mortality rate" is frequently used to describe the rate of death. In this case, the entire population at the time was divided by the number of deaths owing to disease.

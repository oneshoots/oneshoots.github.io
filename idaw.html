Answer:

The Role of Dance

"Dance, when you're broken open. Dance, if you've torn the bandage off. Dance in the middle of fighting. Dance in your blood. Dance when you're perfectly free." - Rumi

Dance is very common ritual across a multitude of cultures. It is a both a form of expression and an art. For the Igorots, they use it to mark the different stages of their life cycles. For instance, each cultural area has a different dance to represent mourning, wedding celebrations, the courtship process, or even ubiquitous activities like the role of a man and woman in society (i.e- women fetching pales of water from the lowlands). Dance is both a celebration, as well as a representation of everyday life. Here is another website that compiles together a list of famous dances by the Igorots.

The Idaw (from a word meaning bird) is a Bontoc dance that portrays a war ceremony done by fighters from rivaling tribes. The dance is named after the bird which was believed to bring the omen of war.

Explanation:

This traditional dance called Idaw is named after a pugnacious and strong bird. The Igorots from the Bontoc region view the Idaw as an allegory for war. This dance symbolizes the beginning of war. The warriors from varying factions take part in this dance. If the warriors were successful in their combat, a ceremonial rite begins. This ritual is often violent, with chieftains of various tribes beheaded. Movements of this dance are both undaunted and intrepid.

Answer:

1. Meaning: The Idaw dance is a warrior dance performed by the Kalinga tribe. It depicts the bravery, strength, and agility of the warriors in battle.

2. Costumes: The dancers wear traditional Kalinga attire, which includes a bahag (loincloth) for men and a tapis (wrap-around skirt) for women. The men also wear a headgear made of animal horns and feathers, while the women wear beads and earrings.

3. Music: The dance is accompanied by the sound of gongs, which are played by a group of musicians. The rhythm and tempo of the music dictate the movements of the dancers.

4. Choreography: The dance involves intricate footwork and hand movements, which are designed to simulate the movements of warriors in battle. The dancers move in synchronized formations, often mimicking the movements of animals such as the eagle and the hawk.

5. Significance: The Idaw dance is an important cultural tradition of the Kalinga tribe, and it is often performed during festivals and other celebrations. It is also considered a form of cultural heritage that is passed down from generation to generation.

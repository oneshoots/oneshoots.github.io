26. Among the poem of Joe Rizal, which one do you like the mot, and cite the pecific tanza that affect you the mot.


I can tell you that Jose Rizal is a national hero of the Philippines and is known for his literary works, including poems, that advocated for social justice, equality, and reform. Some of his most famous poems include "Mi Ultimo Adios" (My Last Farewell) and "El Canto del Viajero" (The Song of the Traveller).

"Mi Ultimo Adios" is a poem that Rizal wrote shortly before his execution in 1896. It is a poignant and moving tribute to his homeland, and is considered one of the greatest love poems in Philippine literature. In the poem, Rizal reflects on the beauty and richness of the Philippine landscape, and expresses his love for his fellow Filipinos and his desire for their freedom and prosperity. The following stanza from the poem is often cited as being particularly powerful and moving:

"Adios, patria adorada,

region del sol querida,

perla del mar de oriente,

nuestro perdido Eden!"

(Farewell, beloved homeland,

beloved region of the sun,

pearl of the eastern sea,

our lost Eden!)

This stanza captures the deep love and longing that Rizal felt for his country and its people, and the sense of loss and sorrow at the prospect of leaving them behind. It is a testament to Rizal's commitment to the cause of Philippine independence and his willingness to sacrifice his life for it.

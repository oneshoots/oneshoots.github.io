The Gettysburg Address is the speech given by former US President Abraham Lincoln. It is to give dedication to the soldiers who risked and sacrificed their lives during the civil war.

The speech alone is a primary source of information but the details inside the address can be considered as secondary. This is because the speech is all about the civil war and the main speaker happens to be former President Abraham Lincoln. The reason why we consider it a primary source of information is that it is directly given by President Abraham Lincoln. It was not altered, nor part of any research paper.

It means 87 years. At the start of the speech, the speaker mentioned about four scores and seven years. This means that there are 20 years in a score and then we would add the 7 years. This would sum up to a total of 87 years. It also refers to the year 1776. The same year when the independence was signed by the founding fathers.

Democracy. In his speech, Lincoln assured us that in the upcoming years, the country and its officers will continue to fight for its freedom. It will continue to serve people the justice and right everyone deserves as what the founding fathers promised on their signed paper. He also promised that the sacrifices made by the soldiers and army will forever be remembered as part of history.

It means that the government shall serve the people because it is the people that make the government possible. the government's role is to manage the country but from Abraham Lincoln's point of view, it is the people that shape the government. Without the cooperation of the people, the government would be a complete waste. It is the people that mandate what will be the future of a government.

Inauguration of a military funeral in Gettysburg, Pennsylvania. The speech was made for the fallen soldiers and to dedicate their heroic actions during the civil war. It was one of the deadliest and bloodiest wars in history so Abraham Lincoln paid tribute to their sacrifices.

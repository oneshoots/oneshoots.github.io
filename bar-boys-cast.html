9. find the measure of the angle of elevation of the sun when a boy 8 feet tall casts a shadow 7 feet long. (Round to the nearest whole degree)


The diagram is in the drawing.

Since the hypotenuse is not given, you will have to use the function tangent (tan = opposite/adjacent). Your equation would look like this: shift tan x = 8/7, wherein shift tan is used because you need to find the angle and x is used to represent the angle of elevation. Since you only need to find the angle, your next equation is: x= shift tan (8/7). Input this in a calculator and you get 48.81407483. Pressing the button that has the degrees and apostrophe symbols on it (usually right next to the button with the word "hyp" on it) will give you the value 48 degrees 48' 51" (actually, you'll get 48 degrees 48' 50.67" but when you round off, you'll get the 51")

note: If you don't understand what is shift tan, then it just means that you need to press the shift button at the upper left corner of the calculator. After you press it, press the "tan" button.

20. Find the angle of elevation of the sun if a boy is 4 ft tall casts a shadow 15 ft long on the level ground. ​


[tex]\LARGE\bold\red{\tt{ANGLE\:OF\: ELEVATION}}[/tex]

PROBLEM:

Find the angle of elevation of the sun if a boy is 4 ft tall casts a shadow 15 ft long on the level ground.

GIVEN:

height (opposite) = 4ft.

distance (adjacent) = 15ft.

angle of depression = ?

SOLUTION:

We use the ratio of tangent

[tex] \large \tt tan \: y = \frac{opposite}{adjacent} \\ \\ \large \tt tan \: y = \frac{4}{15} \\ \\ \large \tt y =tan ^{ - 1} ( \frac{4}{15} ) \\ \\ \large \red{ \boxed{\tt y = 14.93}}[/tex]

CONCLUSION:

∴ The angle of elevation is 14.93 ft.

24. find the angle of elevation of the sun if a boy 5 ft tall casts a shadow of 20 ft long on a level ground?​


Answer:

The angle of elevation of the sun is approximately 14.0362°.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let

θ represent the angle of elevation,y represent the height of the boyx represent the shadow along the ground

Given y = 5 ft and x = 20 ft, we shall calculate the angle of elevation using the inverse of tangent or the arctangent.

[tex] \sf tan\:θ = \frac{opposite}{adjacent} = \frac{y}{x} [/tex]

[tex] \sf tan\:θ = \frac{5}{20} = \frac{1}{4} [/tex]

[tex] \sf θ = arctan\:(\frac{1}{4}) \approx \green{14.0362°} [/tex]

Therefore, the angle of elevation of the sun is approximately 14.0362°.

15. the Philippines statistics authority predicts that the population by the year 2050 will be about 150 million and then will increase by about 0.8 million per year until 2065. represent this given problem as a function where x is the number of years since 2050 find f(8) and explain what it means. ​


Answer:

156,400,000 people

#BRAINLYFAST

Step-by-step explanation:

Population at 2050 = 150,000,000 people

Rate of increase until 2065 = 0.8 million/year or 800,000 people/year

The rate of increase is only available for years 2050 to 2065. 15 years after 2050 only.

To represent this as a function, we can put it as:

f(x) = 150,000,000 + 800,000x

Where:

150,000,000 is the total population in 2050.

x = number of years after year 2050 (NOTE: if x > 15, then the function is not applicable anymore as the given rate of population increase is applicable until year 2065 only.)

To test the function, let's check the number of population in year 2050.

In year 2050, since it's still the same year as the initial population, we can therefore say that x = 0.

f(0) = 150,000,000 + 800,000(0) = 150,000,000 people.

We can see that this is correct since the calculated number of populations was equal to the given number of populations for 2050. (NOTE: This was performed for verification only).

To proceed with the problem, find f(8)

f(8) = 150,000,000 + 800,000(8)

f(8) = 150,000,000 + 6,400,000

f(8) = 156,400,000 people

It means that 8 years after 2050, the population will increase by 6,400,000 people. Additionally, by 2058, 8 years after 2050, the total population will be 156,400,000 people.

Answer:

Approximately (since 0.8 million increase per year is also an approximation), there are 156.479 million people in the year 2058.

Step-by-step explanation:

Since the population is exhibiting linear growth and we have a constant increase yearly, then we can use the exponential growth function.

    Formula:  [tex]f(x) = a(1+r)^{x}[/tex]

    Where;

           a    is the starting value

            r    is the percent growth (usually written as a decimal)

           x     is the number of years

    Therefore;

             a =  150

             x =   8 (given that you want to find the population in 2058)

             r =   0.0053  (How did we get this?) Simply by knowing what part of      

                                   150 is the 0.8 increase per year.

                                   So, 0.8/150 = 0.0053 in decimal, or 0.53%

Plugging them into the formula:

[tex]f(x) = a(1+r)^{x}[/tex]

[tex]f(8)=150(1+0.0053)^{8}[/tex]

[tex]f(8)=150(1.0053)^{8}[/tex]

[tex]f(8)=150(1.04319491258)^{8}[/tex]

[tex]f(8)=156.479[/tex]

Approximately (since 0.8 million increase per year is also an approximation), there are about 156.479 million people in the year 2058.

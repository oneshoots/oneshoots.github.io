4. element of bolinao mats?​


Answer:

A baníg is a handwoven mat usually used in East Asia and the Philippines for sleeping and sitting. This type of mat is traditionally made in the Philippines.

Technically, it is not a textile. Depending on the region of the Philippines, the mat is made of buri[1] (palm), pandanus or sea grass leaves. The leaves are dried, usually dyed, then cut into strips and woven into mats, which may be plain or intricate.[2]

11. A lighthouse 20m high with the base at sea level is sighted by a ship .If the angle of elevation from the ship to the top of the lighthouse is 12degree, what is the distance from the ship to the lighthouse?


Answer:

94 meters

Step-by-step explanation:

What is asked?The distance from the ship to the lighthouse.What are given?20 meters - height of the lighthouse12 degrees - angle of elevationHow to solve?First we will assign variables to the important variables (points and lines) of the problem to make it easier to determine.

A = top of the lighthouse

B= location of the ship

C = base of the lighthouse

a = distance between the lighthouse and the ship

b= height of the lighthouse (20 m)

c = the diagonal sight from the ship to the top of the lighthouse

We also have 12 degrees as the measurement of angle ABC. That means the right angle (90 degrees) is angle ACB and angle BAC measures 78 degrees.

Observing from the data, we can form a right triangle. Because of that, we can use the trigonometric functions. With the known values (b and the angles), we will use the formula:

[tex]tan \theta = \frac{opposite}{adjacent}[/tex], we will use the angle BAC which measures 78 degrees.

Solution:

[tex]tan \theta = \frac{opposite}{adjacent}\\tan 78 = \frac{a}{b}\\tan 78 = \frac{a}{20m}\\a= tan 78 (20m)\\a= (4.7)(20m)\\a= 94 \ meters[/tex]

Answer:

Therefore, the distance from the ship to the lighthouse is 94 meters.

12. What were two jobs of lighthouse keepersbefore lighthouses became automated?​


Answer:

Historically, lighthouse keepers were needed to trim the wicks, replenish fuel, wind clockworks and perform maintenance tasks such as cleaning lenses and windows. They were also responsible for the fog signal and the weather station, and played a major role in search and rescue at sea.

Explanation:

Historically, lighthouse keepers were needed to trim the wicks, replenish fuel, wind clockworks and perform maintenance tasks such as cleaning lenses and windows. They were also responsible for the fog signal and the weather station, and played a major role in search and rescue at sea.

Because most lighthouses are located in remote, isolated or inaccessible areas on islands and coastlines, it was typical for the work of lighthouse keeper to remain within a family, passing from parents to child, all of whom lived in or near the lighthouse itself. "Stag light" was an unofficial term given to some isolated lighthouses in the United States Lighthouse Service. It meant stations that were operated solely by men, rather than accommodating keepers and their families.

Electrification and other technological advancements such as remote monitoring and automatic bulb changing began to appear in the 1960s and over the course of the late 20th century made paid resident keepers at the lights unnecessary in certain areas, while simply altering their responsibilities elsewhere. Those who continue to work as lighthouse keepers today perform building maintenance, repair work to broken and blind buoys, geographic realignment of wayward navigational aids off the coast, and technical maintenance on automated systems. In most countries, the training of lighthouse keepers falls within the jurisdiction of the Navy or Coast guard. In the US, periodic maintenance of the lights is now performed by visiting Coast Guard Aids to Navigation teams.

15. The Hillsboro Inlet Lighthouse lights up how much more area than the Jupiter Inlet Lighthouse? Round your answer to the nearest tenth. Jupiter Inlet Lighthouse- 18 miles. Hillsboro Inlet Lighthouse- 28.


Answer:

The Hillsboro Inlet lighthouse lights up 1444.4 miles² more area than Jupiter Inlet lighthouse.

Step-by-step explanation:

Radius:

Hillsboro Inlet Lighthouse: 28 miles

Jupiter Inlet Lighthouse: 18 miles

The lighthouse lights up an area in circle.

Area of circle = πr²

Where:

π ≈ 3.14

r ⇒ radius

Area each lighthouse covers:

Hillsboro, with 28-mile radius:

Area = (3.14)(28 miles)²

= (3.14)(784 miles²)

≈ 2,461.76  or  2,461.8 miles²  

Jupiter Inlet Lighthouse with 18-mile radius:

Area = (3.14)(18 miles)²

= (3.14)(324 miles²)

≈ 1,017.36 or 1,017.4 miles²

Solve for the area difference to find how much more area the Hillsboro lights up than Jupiter lighthouse:

Area difference = 2,461.8 miles²  - 1,017.4 miles²

Area difference = 1,444.4 miles² (in nearest tenth)

The Hillsboro Inlet lighthouse lights up 1444.4 miles² more area than Jupiter Inlet lighthouse.

25. Three lighthouses flashed simultaneously at 11:00 AM. The first lighthouse flashes every 18 seconds, the second lighthouse flashes every 60 seconds, and the third lighthouse flashes every 66 seconds. Find the next time the three lighthouses flash together again.​


Answer:

Concept

We are looking for the lowest common multiple of 18, 60, and 66.

We can do this by first looking for the lowest common multiple of two of them, and then the result with the third number.

Full explanation

The multiples of 60 are: 60, 120, 180,...

Now 60 is not a multiple of 18, nor is 120, but 180 is.

So the LCM of 18 and 60 is 180.

Now we look for the lowest common multiple of 180 and 66.

180 = 2^2 x 3^2 x 5

66 = 2 x 3 x 11

We take the largest available powers:

2^2 x 3^2 x 5 x 11 = 1980

Thus, the LCM of the three numbers is 1980 second = 33 minutes.

So 33 minutes after 11:00am the lighthouses flash in sync, they will do so again.

Hence, the answer is 11:33am.

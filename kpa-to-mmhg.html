9. 1. A 3.8 moles of gas occupies 75.0 L at a certain temperature. What volume will 7.6 moles of a gas occupy? 2. A 200 mL sample of oxygen is collected when the pressure is 700 mmHg. What volume will the gas occupy at a pressure of 800 mmHg at constant temperature? 3. Find the new volume of a 30 mL gas at 27ºC when the temperature rises to 127ºC at constant pressure. 4. Calculate the pressure exerted by 0.300 mole of a gas contained in an 8.00 L vessel at 18ºC. 5. The volume of a gas at 99.0 kPa is 300.0 ml. If the pressure is increased to 188 kPa, what will be the new volume? ​


1. SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & V_1 = \text{75.0 L} \\ & n_1 = \text{3.8 mol} \\ & n_2 = \text{7.6 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final volume of the gas by using Avogadro's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \frac{V_1}{n_1} & = \frac{V_2}{n_2} \\ n_1V_2 & = V_1n_2 \\ \frac{n_1V_2}{n_1} & = \frac{V_1n_2}{n_1} \\ V_2 & = \frac{V_1n_2}{n_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{75.0 L})(\text{7.6 mol})}{\text{3.8 mol}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{150 L}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 7.6 moles of a gas will occupy a volume of 150 L.

------------------------------------------------------------

2. SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P_1 = \text{700 mmHg} \\ & V_1 = \text{200 mL} \\ & P_2 = \text{800 mmHg} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final volume by using Boyle's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1V_1 & = P_2V_2 \\ P_2V_2 & = P_1V_1 \\ \frac{P_2V_2}{P_2} & = \frac{P_1V_1}{P_2} \\ V_2 & = \frac{P_1V_1}{P_2} \\ & = \frac{(\text{700 mmHg})(\text{200 mL})}{\text{800 mmHg}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{175 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the gas will occupy a volume of 175 mL at 800 mmHg.

------------------------------------------------------------

3. SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

To convert the temperature from degree Celsius to kelvin, add 273 to the temperature expressed in degree Celsius.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & V_1 = \text{30 mL} \\ & T_1 = 27^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{300 K} \\ & T_2 = 127^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{400 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final volume by using Charles' law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \frac{V_1}{T_1} & = \frac{V_2}{T_2} \\ V_2T_1 & = V_1T_2 \\ \frac{V_2T_1}{T_1} & = \frac{V_1T_2}{T_1} \\ V_2 & = \frac{V_1T_2}{T_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{30 mL})(\text{400 K})}{\text{300 K}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{40 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the new volume of the gas is 40 mL.

------------------------------------------------------------

4. SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & V = \text{8.00 L} \\ & n = \text{0.300 mol} \\ & T = 18^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{291.15 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Solve for the pressure of the gas by using ideal gas equation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} PV & = nRT \\ \frac{PV}{V} & = \frac{nRT}{V} \\ P & = \frac{nRT}{V} \\ & = \frac{(\text{0.300 mol})\left(0.082057 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)(\text{291.15 K})}{\text{8.00 L}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{0.896 atm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the pressure exerted by 0.300 mole of a gas is 0.896 atm.

------------------------------------------------------------

5. SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P_1 = \text{99.0 kPa} \\ & V_1 = \text{300.0 mL} \\ & P_2 = \text{188 kPa} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final volume by using Boyle's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1V_1 & = P_2V_2 \\ P_2V_2 & = P_1V_1 \\ \frac{P_2V_2}{P_2} & = \frac{P_1V_1}{P_2} \\ V_2 & = \frac{P_1V_1}{P_2} \\ & = \frac{(\text{99.0 kPa})(\text{300.0 mL})}{\text{188 kPa}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{158.0 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the new volume of the gas will be 158.0 mL.

12. Which conditions are equal to STP? 30°C, 100 kPa –273.15°C, 1 atm 0°C, 760 mmHg 25°C, 101.325 kPa


Conditions are equal to STP : 0 ° C (273.15 K) and 1 atm (101,325 kPa = 760 mmHg)

Further explanation

Standard conditions for temperature and pressure are used as a reference in certain calculations or conditions

There are several standards used by various organizations such as IUPAC or NIST

There are 2 conditions that are usually used as a reference in chemical calculations (mainly for determining the volume per mole of a gas or the molar volume), namely:

1. Standard Conditions

Conditions at T 0 ° C (273.15 K) and P 1 atm are stated by STP (Standard Temperature and Pressure). At STP, Vm is 22.4 liters / mol.

2 Room Condition

Conditions at T 25 ° C (298 K) and P 1 atm are stated by RTP (Room Temperature and Pressure). Vm in this condition = 24.4 liters / mol

So 1 atm if converted to Pascal/mmHg

1 atm = 101,325 kPa = 760 mmHg

So the STP condition is at 0 ° C (273.15 K) and 1 atm (101,325 kPa = 760 mmHg)

17. In a gas mixture composed of oxygen gas, helium, and nitrogen gas, the partial pressure of oxygen is 1 atm, nitrogen is 760 mmHg, and helium is 101.325 kPa. What is the total pressure of the mixture?


Answer:

The total pressure of the gas mixture is 3 atm.

Explanation:

To find the total pressure of the mixture, we need to add up the partial pressures of each gas. However, the partial pressures are given in different units, so we need to convert them all to the same unit. Let's choose atm:

The partial pressure of oxygen is already in atm, so we don't need to do anything: 1 atm.

The partial pressure of nitrogen is given in mmHg, so we need to convert it to atm: 760 mmHg = 1 atm (by definition). Therefore, the partial pressure of nitrogen is also 1 atm.

The partial pressure of helium is given in kPa, so we need to convert it to atm: 101.325 kPa = 0.999972 atm (using the conversion factor 1 kPa = 0.00986923 atm). Therefore, the partial pressure of helium is approximately 1 atm (to three decimal places).

Now we can add up the partial pressures to find the total pressure of the mixture:

Total pressure = partial pressure of oxygen + partial pressure of nitrogen + partial pressure of helium

Total pressure = 1 atm + 1 atm + 1 atm

Total pressure = 3 atm

Therefore, the total pressure of the gas mixture is 3 atm.

29. a ballon contains 6.8 L of oxygen the pressure is reduced to 2.50 atm thus allowing the balloon to expand and occupy 37.3 L of space what was the initial pressure exerted on the balloon in mmHg? in kPa​


Answer:

P1 = 1.04 × 10⁴ mm Hg

P1 = 1.39 × 10³ kPa

Explanation:

Use Boyle's Law to solve for the initial pressure.

P1V1 = P2V2

P1 = P2 • (V2 / V1)

P1 = 2.50 atm • (37.3 L / 6.8 L)

P1 = 13.7 atm

Convert pressure in mm Hg.

P1 = 13.7 atm × (760 mm Hg / 1 atm)

P1 = 1.04 × 10⁴ mm Hg

Convert pressure in kPa.

P1 = 13.7 atm × (101.325 kPa / 1 atm)

P1 = 1.39 × 10³ kPa

30. 1. The total pressure in a closed container of three mixed gases is 96.4 kPa. The partial pressure of hydrogen in the mixture is 13.5 kPa and the partial pressure of oxygen is 29.3 kPa. The third gas in the mixture is methane, what is its partial pressure? 2. Oxygen and chlorine gas are mixed in a container with partial pressures of 401 mmHg and 0.639 atm, respectively. What is the total pressure inside the container (in atm)?


Problem 1:Given:

[tex]P_{\text{T}} = \text{96.4 kPa}[/tex]

[tex]P_{\text{H}_{2}} = \text{13.5 kPa}[/tex]

[tex]P_{\text{O}_{2}} = \text{29.3 kPa}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]P_{\text{CH}_{4}}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]P_{\text{T}} = P_{\text{H}_{2}} + P_{\text{O}_{2}} + P_{\text{CH}_{4}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{\text{CH}_{4}} = P_{\text{T}} - P_{\text{H}_{2}} - P_{\text{O}_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{\text{CH}_{4}} = \text{96.4 kPa} - \text{13.5 kPa} - \text{29.3 kPa}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P_{\text{CH}_{4}} = \text{53.6 kPa}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{\text{O}_{2}} = \text{401 mmHg} × \frac{\text{1 atm}}{\text{760 mmHg}} = \text{0.5276 atm}[/tex]

[tex]P_{\text{Cl}_{2}} = \text{0.639 atm}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]P_{\text{T}} = P_{\text{O}_{2}} + P_{\text{Cl}_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{\text{T}} = \text{0.5276 atm} + \text{0.639 atm}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P_{\text{T}} = \text{1.167 atm}}[/tex]

8. construct a one week training plan to improve table tennis skills for backhand


Here is a sample one week training plan to improve backhand skills in table tennis:

Day 1:

Warm up by stretching and doing some light exercises

Practice footwork drills to improve your movement around the table

Focus on backhand drills using a robot or a partner feeding balls to your backhand side

Work on adjusting the angle of your racket and the timing of your stroke to hit different types of backhand shots (e.g. topspin, flat, lob)

End the session with some light cardio to cool down

Day 2:

Warm up with stretching and light exercises

Practice footwork drills to improve your movement around the table

Work on your backhand serves, focusing on spin, placement, and disguise

Practice receiving backhand serves, using different types of backhand shots to return the serve

End the session with some light cardio to cool down

Day 3:

Warm up with stretching and light exercises

Practice footwork drills to improve your movement around the table

Work on your backhand loop, focusing on generating power and spin

Practice returning backhand loops from a partner or robot

End the session with some light cardio to cool down

Day 4:

Warm up with stretching and light exercises

Practice footwork drills to improve your movement around the table

Work on your backhand push, focusing on placement and spin

Practice returning backhand pushes from a partner or robot

End the session with some light cardio to cool down

Day 5:

Warm up with stretching and light exercises

Practice footwork drills to improve your movement around the table

Work on your backhand counterhit, focusing on timing and placement

Practice returning backhand counterhits from a partner or robot

End the session with some light cardio to cool down

Day 6:

Warm up with stretching and light exercises

Practice footwork drills to improve your movement around the table

Work on your backhand chop, focusing on spin and placement

Practice returning backhand chops from a partner or robot

End the session with some light cardio to cool down

Day 7:

Warm up with stretching and light exercises

Practice footwork drills to improve your movement around the table

Review and practice all the backhand shots and skills you have worked on throughout the week

End the session with some light cardio to cool down

It's important to remember to warm up before each training session and to cool down afterwards to prevent injuries. You should also vary the types of drills and shots you practice to keep things interesting and to improve your overall game. Finally, make sure to get plenty of rest and hydration to allow your body to recover between training sessions.

20. How will you improve your skills in backhand drive for table tennis?


Answer:

The Stance

Feet should be slightly wider than shoulder width apart.

Stance is “square to the line of play”, basically your feet should be pointing in the direction you are hitting the ball.

Knees should be slightly bent.

Body should be leaning forward.

Both arms should be out in front of you.

About a 90-110 degree bend at the elbow.

Stand quite close to the table, an arms length away.

Weight distributed on both feet and on the balls/toes, not heels.

The Backswing

Bring your bat backwards and down to somewhere just in front of your belly button.

Create a slightly closed bat angle.

Keep your wrist straight

The backhand rubber should be pointing in the direction you wish to play.

The Strike

The arm moves forwards, to meet the ball, and slightly up, to create a bit of topspin.

The movement comes predominantly from the elbow and forearm.

Bat angle stays closed throughout the shot.

Take the ball at the peak of the bounce and out in front of you.

Keep a small gap between the elbow and the body.

The Finish

Follow through, forward and upward.

Your bat should finish pointing where you have hit the ball, roughly at about chin level.

Don’t let your arms swing across your body too much to the right.

Always get back to the ready position.

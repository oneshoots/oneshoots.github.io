1. How can you find the exact center of any unmarked circle such as a coin​


Step-by-step explanation:

STEP 1: DRAW A CHORD ACROSS THE CIRCLE

Draw a line across the circle near the edge so it cuts the circumference in two places. This is called a chord.

If you can also make the chord a nice easy length i.e. 10, 20, 24, etc this might make life easier in the next step.

STEP 2: FIND THE MID POINT OF THE CHORD

Draw a line perpendicular to the chord, half way along it's length.

Now you know why it's easier to pick an easy length to start with.

Make sure it goes past where the center of the circle should be. You can go straight across if that's easier.

STEP 3: REPEAT STEP 2 FOR ANOTHER CHORD

Repeat step 2 for another chord.

This should be enough to find the center of the circle, but you can add more if needed.

STEP 4: USE MORE CHORDS FOR ACCURACY

If you're not that confident of your measurements, then you can use as many chords as you like, until you're happy with the result.

Once you have the center marked you can use this information for other things, like drilling a hole in the middle, drawing concentric rings etc.

5. What is the exact location of all the atoms in the universe at this exact moment?​


Answer:

The answer is mostly the sun.

Explanation:

Most atoms are located in stars and gas clouds. The sun contains about half of the universe's mass, and most of its atoms--including 98% of the galaxies' hydrogen atoms--are in the sun's outer layers.

There are also lots of atoms in the empty space between stars and galaxies--this is known as the interstellar medium.

There are billions of other galaxies with billions of other stars. So, while the sun makes up most of the mass of the universe, it does not contain all of the atoms, nor is it the only thing where atoms are located.

17. 3. Teacher Cindy wants to measure the ALS Community Learning Center (CLC). The room floor is n exact square and the one side measures 3 meters. What is the perimeter of the CLC?​


Answer:

12 Meters

Step-by-step explanation:

If one side of the room measures 3 meters, and the room is an exact square, then all sides of the room must also measure 3 meters.

To find the perimeter of the room, we need to add up the lengths of all four sides. Since all four sides have the same length of 3 meters, we can multiply 3 by 4 to get the total perimeter:

Perimeter = 4 x 3 meters

Perimeter = 12 meters

Therefore, the perimeter of the ALS Community Learning Center (CLC) is 12 meters.

13. Which of the following sequences correctly lists the different arrivals from first to last?

Answer:

C. P waves -S waves - Surface waves

Explanation:

P-waves, also known as primary waves or pressure waves, travel at the greatest velocity through the Earth.

S-waves, also known as secondary waves, shear waves or shaking waves, are transverse waves that travel slower than P-waves.

Surface waves are similar in nature to water waves and travel just under the Earth’s surface. They are typically generated when the source of the earthquake is close to the Earth’s surface. Although surface waves travel more slowly than S-waves, they can be much larger in amplitude and can be the most destructive type of seismic wave.

20. A boat tied to a post is rocked by waves 12 m apart and with a speed of 3 m/s. What is the frequency of the wave?​


FREQUENCY

Problem:

A boat tied to a post is rocked by waves 12 m apart and with a speed of 3 m/s. What is the frequency of the wave?

Answer:The frequency of the wave is 0.25 Hz.

_ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _

Equation:[tex] \underline{ \boxed{f = \frac{v}{ \lambda} }}[/tex]

where,

[tex] \sf \red {f } \: \: is \: the \: frequency[/tex][tex] \sf \red {v } \: \: is \: the \: velocity, \: and;[/tex][tex] \sf \red {\lambda} \: \: is \: the \: wavelength[/tex]

- Using the formula above, to calculate the frequency of the wave. We just need to substitute the givens to the equation, then solve.

Solution:

Since the givens are,

speed / velocity = 3 m/swavelength = 12 m

so,

[tex] \: f = \frac{v}{ \lambda} \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \sf \: f = \frac{3 \: m/s}{ 12 \: m} \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: = \sf \underline{ \boxed{ \green{ \sf0.25 \: Hz}}}[/tex]

Therefore, the frequency of the wave is 0.25 Hz.

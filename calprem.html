What are ceramics made of?

A ceramic is an inorganic (non-living, non-hydrocarbon), non-metallic substance, So plastics, metals and alloys, living tissue, etc. are non-ceramic.

Common ceramics would be tableware made of china, pottery, bricks, and many floor and wall tiles. Not-well-known ceramics include many small electronic components, such as capacitors, transistors, laser crystals, and others.

Most of the earth’s crust is composed of silicate rock, which is a type of natural ceramic. Sand is silicon dioxide, which qualifies as a ceramic (non-metallic, inorganic).

2. Debra has two different square baking dishes. One has a side length of 12 cm and the other has a side length of 20 cm. What is the difference in the area of Debra's two square baking dishes?​


In the given problem, apply your knowledge in algebraic expression and basic mathematics especially in solving word problem about area of squares.

Given

One has a side length of 12 cm and the other has a side length of 20 cm.

Problem

What is the difference in the area of Debra's two square baking dishes?

Solution

Use the formula for finding area of squares A = s²

Area of First Baking Dishes

s = 12 cm

A = 12² cm²

A = 144 cm²

Area of Second Baking Dishes

s = 20 cm

A = 20² cm²

A = 400 cm²

Difference Area of Two Baking Dishes

Difference = 400 cm² - 144 cm²

Difference = 256 cm²

Answer

256 cm²

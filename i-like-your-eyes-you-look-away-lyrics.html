27. I have dimples right under my eyes. It looks like whiskers. Is it genetically inherited?


Dimples are genetically inherited.

Dimples are facial features which are caused by a facial muscle called as the zygomaticus major which is involved in facial expression. This muscle is the one helping in raising the corners of the mouth when smiling. For normal people, this muscle begins at the bone in the cheek,called the zygomatic bone, running downwards connecting it to the mouth. For those who are with dimples, the said muscle divides into separate bundles of muscle running into the mouth.

They are considered often as a birth defect but without a health effect.

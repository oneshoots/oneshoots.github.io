9. A 20.50 mL sample of gas is at 5.167atm. What will be the volume if the pressure becomes 1.500 atm, with a fixed amount of gas and temperature. (V2= P1V1/P2)​


BOYLE'S LAW

A 20.50 mL sample of gas is at 5.167atm. What will be the volume if the pressure becomes 1.500 atm, with a fixed amount of gas and temperature.

So, the problem want us to solve for the new volume. So, we're going to use Boyle's Law Equation.

[tex] \boxed{\bold{V_2 = \frac{P_1V_1}{P_2}}}[/tex]

We have the givens, based on the problem.

P1 = 5.167 atmP2 = 1.500 atmV1 = 20.50 mLV2 = ?

Plug in the givens

[tex] V_2 = \frac{(5.167)(20.50)}{1.500} [/tex][tex] V_2 = \frac{105.9235}{1.500} [/tex][tex] V_2 = 70.61 [/tex]

The new volume of the sample gas is 70.61 mL

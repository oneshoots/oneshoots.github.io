4. what is the difference between physical freedom and moral freedom?​


Answer:

Moral freedom

Moral freedom is not the absence of moral restraint nor the freedom to live your life without morality. Moral freedom is the freedom to choose a set of moral values and to live your life in accordance with those values.

An example of moral freedom is a person choosing to live his or her life bound by adherence to virtues such as honesty, loyalty, forgiveness, and self-discipline.

Living within such a moral framework provides freedom to act without fear or uncertainty when faced with difficult decisions. Every decision made is tested against the virtues that form this moral framework.

Am I being honest?

Am I being loyal?

Am I being forgiving?

Am I exercising self-discipline?

Without such a framework to test against, our lives can become an agony of indecision and doubt. We can be held prisoner by doubt and uncertainty and real freedom comes not from an absence of restraint but from a clearly marked path to follow.

Physical freedom

Physical freedom is about possibility; travel, adventure, doing fun things with my family and friends, being excited and engaged with my work. It’s about being able to hike up the mountains to see the gorillas, getting through my workday, where I sit a lot, without aches and pains, lifting my bags into the overhead storage compartment with ease, being excited about implementing a new idea and having the energy to kick it up a gear when I need to.

23. Examples of physical freedom


Physical freedom can be defined as the freedom that a person has in using his physic or his body and determining what is good for his physic or body without any interference or coercion from other people.

Examples of physical freedom are:

1. Freedom to move.

This is the most basic physical freedom. If a person is unable to move, for example, because they are handcuffed, bound, or restricted, then they are said to have no physical freedom. To have physical freedom, one must be free to move.

2. Freedom to use the physical in various activities.

This activity can be any activity, such as climbing, dancing, playing ball, and other activities. If a person cannot do physical activity according to what he wants, then he does not have physical freedom.

3. Freedom to determine what is best for his physique.

For example:

Doing a diet program.Go to the gym three times a week to exercise.Take vitamins and other decisions related to physical.

What needs to be remembered is that this physical freedom must be accompanied by the awareness that being free does not mean we can do everything, we still have to ensure that what we do to or with our physic is truly positive and does not destroy or harm ourselves.

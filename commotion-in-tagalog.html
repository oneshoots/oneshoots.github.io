15. are the factors that affect the rate of chemical reactions useful to you especially when dealing with fire control commotion, and pollution? how? give your explanation​


Answer:

Catalysts.

Without them many reactions would be slow or non-existant.

One example is when Platinum is incorporated into vehicle exhaust systems to facilitate further reduction of exhaust gas pollutants with oxygen before they’re released into our atmosphere.

Explanation

There are quite a few factors which affect the rate of a chemical reaction. Some of these are:

Temperature: It is commonly observed that increasing the temperature of the system increases the rate of the reaction greatly. Rates of some reactions even double or triple when the temperature is increased by 10°C!

State of the reactant: Gaseous reactants show the fastest rate of reaction due to high energy from random movement and tendency to get adsorbed on the surface of the reactant.

Finely-divided and powdered reactants in case of solid state reactions are found to give maximum rate as the surface area available for the reaction to occur is increased by many times.

The presence of a positive catalyst increases the rate of the reaction by altering the mechanism, and thus, changing the path of the reaction. Some reactions which take hours to complete are completed in minutes in the presence of catalysts.

Concentration of the reactants: More concentrated reactants give a higher rate of reaction as the are a greater number of reactant molecules present for the reaction to proceed.
